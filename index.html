Seong Woo Han

I’m a Ph.D. student in computer and information science at the University of Pennsylvania advised by Yoseph Barash, where I’m in the BioCiphers group. My research interests lie in probabilistic machine learning methods with a particular interest in the context of genomics and drug discovery.

Previously, I was a computational biologist at Memorial Sloan Kettering Institute, working with Quaid Morris. I completed my undergraduate studies in Mathematics at NYU, where I worked with Charlie Peskin and Charles Puelz at the Courant Institute of Mathematical Sciences.
